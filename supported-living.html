Our Services

Supported Living

Andersen Care provides supported living services and accommodation for vulnerable adults.

What We Offer

Supported Living Services

Andersen Care provides supported living services and accommodation. Our accommodation includes shared houses, studio flats, 1 bedroom flats, 2 bedroom flats, en-suite accommodation. All our homes are furnished; shared houses are also available for vulnerable adults within the local communities and several London Boroughs.

We provide supported living to people who are either with particular complex needs, including mental health issues; living by themselves with support or share an ordinary or purpose built home with a small number of other vulnerable or disabled people; people who are semi independent; or people who are autistic or suffer from learning disabilities.

Our Clients

Who We Support

Andersen Care will also support individuals who have developed more independent living skills and who don't need full residential support whilst living in a shared environment.

Complex Needs & Mental Health

Individuals with particular complex needs, including mental health issues, who require tailored support in a shared or independent living environment.

Shared Living

People living by themselves with support or sharing an ordinary or purpose built home with a small number of other vulnerable or disabled people.

Semi-Independent Living

People who are semi-independent and developing skills for greater autonomy while still benefiting from structured support.

Autism & Learning Disabilities

People who are autistic or suffer from learning disabilities, receiving person-centred support tailored to their specific needs.

Our Approach

Person-Centred Support

At Andersen Care we use a person-centred approach where support is tailored to each individual’s needs.

The focus is on promoting independence and enabling individuals to live meaningful, active lives in their communities.

Every individual receives a tailored support plan based on their personal goals, choices, and aspirations.

Comprehensive Support

Support We Provide

The support provided will include all aspects of a person's life from their health and well-being to education and skills development.

Health & Well-being

Comprehensive support for physical and mental health needs, including access to medical and dental services.

Education & Skills Development

Support for vocational or educational activities, helping individuals develop life skills for independent living.

Day Activities & Employment

Encouraging participation in day activities and employment opportunities with a focus on developing and maintaining relationships.

Social Integration

Supporting individuals to integrate into society and maintain their circle of friends and community connections.

Our Team

Staff Support Levels

There will be an established level of staff support. Our staff will either:

Scheduled Visits

Visit the person on a scheduled basis, providing regular check-ins and support as needed.

24-Hour Presence

Offer 24-hour presence for those requiring continuous support and supervision.

Supported Housing

Provision of support-based housing with no personal care requirements.

Youth Support

Semi-Independent Living

Andersen Care provides a high quality and supportive atmosphere for Semi-Independent living that will enable teenagers and young adults to develop and thrive towards bright independent futures.

Our Service

The Andersen Care semi-independent Living service is geared towards looking after young people and making a smooth transition from care to independence for them. We pride ourselves on providing a service that young people will be able to relate to and understand.

Our semi-independent living provisions have 24 hours on-site support and staff cover. The aim of supported accommodation is to give individuals the opportunity to live as independently as possible with help from our qualified and trained staff.

Our clients make their own choices and lead their lives in the way they want to, whenever possible. Our clients have tenancy agreements and are given the same rights as anyone living in his/her own accommodation.

Development

Life Skills Programme

We offer programmes that are designed to incorporate many of the skills needed for a successful transition towards independent living.

Basic Home Maintenance

Use of electricity, gas, water, etc.

Money Matters

Paying bills, budgeting, saving.

Healthy Living

Buying healthy products and preparing a healthy diet.

Emergency Preparedness

Dealing with emergencies - basic first aid, power cuts, leaks, fire, etc.

Accessing Services

Benefits, job centres, education etc.

Social Skills

How to interact respectfully and positively with those around you.

Self Esteem

How to feel positive about yourself and your future.

Awareness

Alcohol and drugs awareness.

Current Affairs

Taking an interest in your community and issues that affect you.

Ongoing Support

Aftercare

Andersen Care recognises that a smooth transition from dependence to self-support is a continuing process.

Andersen Care will work with the Local Leaving Care team to ensure that this process is managed appropriately. Aftercare assistance that may be available to the young person, based on assessed needs, include:

  • Counselling and Support Worker Support
  • Support for Vocational or Educational Activities
  • Professional Therapy
  • Legal Advice and Representation
  • Medical and Dental
