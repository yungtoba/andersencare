Respite Care Services

What is Respite Home Care?

Respite home care provides temporary support for individuals who rely on regular care, offering short‑term assistance when a primary carer—whether a family member, loved one or professional care assistant—needs time away. This may be due to annual leave, holidays, personal commitments, illness or other unforseen circumstances.

Many carers feel anxious about taking a break, but respite care ensures continuity of support, allowing your loved one to remain in the comfort and familiarity of their own home while their needs continue to be met safely and compassionately.

In urgent situations, Andersen Care can also provide emergency respite care, subject to availability.

Adult Care Support Services

Andersen Care provides personalised support for adults aged 18 and above, helping individuals live as independently as possible while receiving care that is tailored to their needs, routines, and goals. Adult care can include daily support, short-term or long-term assistance, live-in care, and supported independent living. These services are designed to promote personal development, well-being, social participation, education, and day-to-day independence.

Types of Adult Care Support

Live-in and At-Home Care:Andersen Care professionals can provide one-to-one support for adults with physical health, mental health, or day-to-day living needs. This allows individuals to remain in familiar surroundings while maintaining comfort, safety, and independence.

Specialist Support for Adults: Our adult care services support a wide range of complex needs, including neurological conditions, physical disabilities, learning disabilities, spinal injuries, and recovery from complex injuries. Each support plan is designed around the individual to ensure the right level of practical help, dignity, and independence.
